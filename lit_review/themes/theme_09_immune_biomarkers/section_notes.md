## Anchor Studies

- **Rizvi et al. (2015)** [PMID: 25765070] [10.1126/science.aaa1348](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aaa1348)
  *Science (New York, N.Y.)* — Cancer immunology. Mutational landscape determines sensitivity to PD-1 blockade in non-small cell lung cancer.
  TLDR: Treatment efficacy was associated with a higher number of mutations in the tumors, and a tumor-specific T cell response paralleled tumor regression in one patient, suggesting that the genomic landscape of lung cancers shapes response to anti–PD-1 therapy.

- **Thorsson et al. (2018)** [PMID: 29628290] [10.1016/j.immuni.2018.03.023](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.immuni.2018.03.023)
  *Immunity* — The Immune Landscape of Cancer.
  TLDR: An extensive immunogenomic analysis of more than 10,000 tumors comprising 33 diverse cancer types by utilizing data compiled by TCGA found six immune subtypes-wound healing, IFN-γ dominant, inflammatory, lymphocyte depleted, immunologically quiet, and TGF-β dominant-characterized by differences in macrophage or lymphocyte signatures.

- **Reynolds et al. (2021)** [PMID: 33479125] [10.1126/science.aba6500](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aba6500)
  *Science (New York, N.Y.)* — Developmental cell programs are co-opted in inflammatory skin disease.
  TLDR: A comprehensive atlas of human skin in early prenatal life, in adulthood, and during inflammatory skin disease is generated, suggesting that a fetal skin signature is expressed in adult inflamed skin and that inflammatory skin diseases reactivate a developmental cell program.

- **Bagaev et al. (2021)** [PMID: 34019806] [10.1016/j.ccell.2021.04.014](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ccell.2021.04.014)
  *Cancer cell* — Conserved pan-cancer microenvironment subtypes predict response to immunotherapy.
  TLDR: The TME subtypes act as a generalized immunotherapy biomarker across many cancer types due to the inclusion of malignant and microenvironment components, and a visual tool integrating transcriptomic and genomic data provides a global tumor portrait.

## What the literature establishes

Immune biomarkers for immunotherapy response prediction in NSCLC extend well beyond PD-L1 expression. Tumor mutational burden (TMB) correlates with immunotherapy benefit but has limitations in its predictive value, particularly in specific molecular contexts (e.g., STK11/KEAP1 co-mutated tumors respond poorly despite adequate TMB).

Pan-cancer immune landscape analyses have classified tumors into immune subtypes with distinct biological properties and treatment implications. Gene expression signatures reflecting T cell inflammation and interferon signaling have shown reproducible associations with immunotherapy response across tumor types. The gut microbiome has emerged as a modulator of immunotherapy efficacy.

## Where consensus exists

- PD-L1 alone is an imperfect predictor of immunotherapy response
- TMB provides additional but imperfect predictive value
- STK11/KEAP1 co-mutations confer immunotherapy resistance in NSCLC
- Multi-parameter biomarker approaches outperform single markers

## Where the field disagrees or is uncertain

- Optimal TMB threshold for clinical decision-making
- Whether composite biomarkers significantly outperform individual markers in practice
- The degree to which microbiome modulation can improve immunotherapy outcomes
- How to standardize HLA typing for immunotherapy prediction

## Gaps and open questions

- Composite biomarker panels not yet standardized for clinical use
- Spatial immune biomarkers (e.g., immune proximity scores) not validated prospectively
- Biomarker dynamics during treatment poorly captured
- Microbiome-based immunotherapy prediction requires larger validation cohorts
- Neoantigen-based personalized approaches need outcome data
